# E-Commerce Customer Segmentation using RFM and Clustering
Aim is to clean the data, engineer customer-level features, perform exploratory analysis, and segment customers using clustering.

## Project Overview
This project aims to understand customer behavior, target marketing efforts more effectively, and improve customer retention for an e-commerce business. By applying RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis and K-Means clustering, we segment customers into distinct groups, allowing for tailored strategies for each segment.

## Business Problem
The core business problem is to gain deeper insights into the customer base of an online retail business. Specifically, the goal is to:
- Identify different customer segments based on their purchasing habits.
- Develop targeted marketing strategies for each segment.
- Improve customer retention by addressing the needs of various customer groups.
- Maximize customer lifetime value.

## Dataset Description
The analysis uses the `ecommerce_customer_segmentation.xlsx` dataset. This transaction-level dataset includes:
- **`InvoiceNo`**: Unique identifier for each transaction. Starts with 'C' for cancellations.
- **`StockCode`**: Product (item) code.
- **`Description`**: Product (item) name.
- **`Category`**: Product category.
- **`Quantity`**: The quantity of each product per transaction.
- **`InvoiceDate`**: Date and time of the transaction.
- **`UnitPrice`**: Unit price of the product.
- **`CustomerID`**: Unique identifier for each customer. Missing values indicate guest checkouts.
- **`Country`**: Country where the customer resides.

Each row represents a single item within a transaction. The dataset originates from an online retail business.

## Data Cleaning Summary
Robust data cleaning was performed to ensure the quality of the analysis:
- **Missing Customer IDs**: 208 rows with missing `CustomerID` were dropped as they are crucial for customer segmentation.
- **Missing Product Descriptions**: 64 rows with missing `Description` were dropped as product details are vital.
- **Negative/Zero Quantities**: 183 rows with `Quantity <= 0` were removed, typically indicating returns or invalid entries.
- **Zero/Negative Unit Prices**: 61 rows with `UnitPrice <= 0` were removed as they represent invalid sales.
- **Cancelled Invoices**: No cancelled invoices (InvoiceNo starting with 'C') were found in the remaining data, so no rows were removed at this stage.
- **Duplicate Records**: 31 duplicate rows were identified and removed.
- **Data Types**: All data types were confirmed to be appropriate for analysis after cleaning.

## Feature Engineering Summary
New features were engineered to prepare the data for RFM analysis and customer-level insights:
- **`Revenue`**: Calculated as `Quantity * UnitPrice` for each transaction line item.
- **Customer-Level Aggregations**: Aggregated features per `CustomerID`:
  - `TotalRevenue`: Sum of revenue for each customer.
  - `TotalPurchases`: Number of unique invoices for each customer (Frequency).
  - `TotalQuantity`: Total quantity of items purchased.
  - `UniqueProducts`: Number of distinct products purchased.
  - `Country`: Most frequent country of purchase.
  - `AverageOrderValue`: `TotalRevenue / TotalPurchases`.
- **RFM Table (`rfm_df`)**: Created with `CustomerID` as the index, including:
  - `Recency`: Days since the customer's last purchase relative to the day after the latest transaction in the dataset.
  - `Frequency`: Number of unique purchases (invoices).
  - `Monetary`: Total revenue generated by the customer.

## Clustering Approach
K-Means clustering was applied to segment customers based on their RFM features:
- **Feature Selection**: `Recency`, `Frequency`, and `Monetary` features from `rfm_df` were selected.
- **Scaling**: Features were scaled using `StandardScaler` to ensure that no single feature dominates the clustering process.
- **Optimal K Determination**: The Elbow Method was used to identify the optimal number of clusters. The plot indicated that `k=3` was the most suitable choice.
  ![Elbow Method for Optimal K](path/to/elbow_method_plot.png)
- **Model Training**: A K-Means model was trained with 3 clusters, and cluster labels were assigned to both `rfm_df` and `customer_features`.
- **Visualization**: Clusters were visualized in 2D using Principal Component Analysis (PCA) for dimensionality reduction.
  ![K-Means Clustering Visualization (2D PCA)](path/to/pca_clustering_plot.png)

## Cluster Interpretation
Three distinct customer segments were identified, each with unique characteristics:

### Cluster 0: Recent, Frequent, and Mid-Value Customers (Loyal Customers)
- **Characteristics**: Moderate recency, high frequency, mid-range monetary value.
- **Interpretation**: These customers are engaged and contribute consistently to revenue. They are a stable source of business.

### Cluster 1: Highly Recent, Very Frequent, and High-Value Customers (High-Value Loyal Customers / Champions)
- **Characteristics**: Very low recency (most recent purchases), very high frequency, high monetary value.
- **Interpretation**: This is the most valuable and crucial segment for the business, representing the 'champions' who are highly engaged and spend significantly.

### Cluster 2: Less Recent, Less Frequent, and Low-Value Customers (At-Risk / One-Time Buyers)
- **Characteristics**: High recency (haven't purchased recently), low frequency, low monetary value.
- **Interpretation**: This segment includes customers who are becoming inactive or were one-time buyers. They represent an opportunity for re-engagement or are at risk of churning.

## Final Business Recommendations
Actionable strategies were developed for each customer segment:

### Cluster 0: Loyal Customers
- **Recommendation**: Focus on retention and increasing average order value.
- **Strategy**: Implement loyalty programs, offer personalized product recommendations, provide exclusive access to new products, and incentivize slightly higher spending.

### Cluster 1: High-Value Loyal Customers / Champions
- **Recommendation**: Nurture and reward these customers to maintain their loyalty and advocacy.
- **Strategy**: Provide VIP treatment (dedicated support, early access), offer personalized thank-you gifts, encourage brand advocacy through referral programs, and seek their feedback.

### Cluster 2: At-Risk / One-Time Buyers
- **Recommendation**: Implement re-engagement campaigns and understand their barriers to repeat purchases.
- **Strategy**: Send targeted re-engagement emails with personalized offers, conduct surveys to understand inactivity, offer limited-time promotions, and consider 'win-back' campaigns with significant discounts.
